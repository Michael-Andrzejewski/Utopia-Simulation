# Eunomia , 2020-2025 Scenario Exploration

*Optimistic and pessimistic runs of Michael's first 5-year agenda. These are explorations, not canon. The adjudicated realistic outcome is recorded as canon in `../timeline.md`.*

The agenda under test (2020-2025): "10 strikes" criminal-justice reform + $5B + an ACX-style research unit; Abundance housing/HSR via a red-tape strike team and tiered builder rewards; hardened fare gates ("travel will be free, but rules are real"); corporate incentive alignment; and the Yudkowsky nested-delegation pyramid under a retained dictatorship.

---

## OPTIMISTIC scenario (2020-2025)

Five years that prove decisive, competent governance can move a rich-but-dysfunctional place fast.

**Criminal justice.** The quantitative research unit ($5B, real data access) quickly confirms what the evidence already suggested: certainty beats severity, a small number of high-rate offenders drive most harm, and reentry, addiction treatment, and mental health are the highest-leverage spends. The "10 strikes" framing , a published, graduated ladder with genuine off-ramps and rehabilitation , reads as *fair and predictable*, which is exactly what deters. By 2025 crime is down, the incarcerated population is smaller but better-targeted, and trust rises because the rules are legible and enforced evenly. Violence and incarceration are both major QALY destroyers, so the bottom of the distribution lifts.

**Abundance / housing.** The red-tape strike team is the crown jewel. Replacing objection-driven permitting with fast, rules-based approval, and paying builders tiered bonuses for speed *and* inspected quality, sends housing starts surging. Homelessness , overwhelmingly a housing-cost problem , falls hard for the first time in a generation as supply outpaces demand and supportive housing scales. Construction becomes Eunomia's booming backbone. High-speed rail, freed from veto-by-committee, breaks ground and lays real track. Housing security is one of the largest wellbeing levers in a rich society, so this is the big mover.

**Enforcement and order.** Hardened fare gates plus the honest message ("travel will get cheaper, even free, but the rules are real") restore order on transit; ridership and perceived safety climb. It becomes the visible emblem , laws mean something , and that credibility makes every other policy easier.

**Governance.** The nested pyramid (50-200 citizens , representative , delegate , elector , council of nine) channels real local knowledge upward on housing and town issues while keeping decisions coherent. People feel heard *and* see results, so legitimacy is high despite the dictatorship. Clear, enforced rules plus outcome-based rewards curb the usual graft.

**Net by 2025:** crime down, homelessness down, housing abundant and cheaper, transit orderly, institutions trusted, economy strong. Absolute QALYs (already high in California) tick up; diff-from-expected is strongly positive, because the real California trajectory was stagnation on exactly these issues. Eunomia becomes a model others want to copy , good for the world points.

## PESSIMISTIC scenario (2020-2025)

The same plan, but friction, backlash, and the hard limits of even a benevolent dictatorship inside the US bite.

**Criminal justice.** "10 strikes" is attacked from both sides , too soft (ten chances for repeat offenders) and too novel , and a single high-profile crime by someone mid-ladder dominates the news. The research unit is real but slow; good policy takes years to show results and five isn't enough, so the public sees the $5B cost before the recidivism benefit. Uneven early enforcement undercuts the "fair and predictable" promise.

**Abundance / housing.** Cutting red tape collides with **federal law a California dictator can't waive** (NEPA, Clean Water Act, Endangered Species Act, federal courts), plus lawsuits and entrenched interests. A few fast-tracked projects hit quality or environmental problems that vindicate the objectors. The builder-bonus system gets gamed , firms chase speed bonuses and cut corners, or capture inspectors. In a frozen-tech world, construction labor and materials are a genuine bottleneck, so the boom is more of a bump. Homelessness improves only modestly; HSR overruns again.

**Enforcement and order.** The fare crackdown reads as punitive and regressive , real money spent policing poor riders for small sums , and clashes with the "travel should be free" rhetoric (why gates now?). Minor in QALY terms, but a credibility ding.

**Governance.** The novel pyramid confuses people; turnout and understanding are low; in practice the council of nine is a rubber stamp, so it's seen as legitimacy theater. Concentrating power in one permanent dictator worries allies and citizens, and "fewer but stricter, enforced laws" produces harsh, unjust edge cases that erode goodwill.

**Net by 2025:** real but modest gains, overshadowed by backlash, federal friction, and the gap between announcement and result. Absolute QALYs roughly hold (California was already high); diff-from-expected is mildly positive at best. The model looks contested rather than triumphant, so the world points are muted.

# Bangladesh , 2020-2025 Scenario Exploration

*Optimistic and pessimistic runs. The canon realistic adjudication lives in `../timeline.md`.*

## OPTIMISTIC

The community-health-worker network scales to near-universal coverage fast: immunization ~99%, child and maternal mortality plunge, drowning and stunting programs land hard, tobacco use drops sharply, the graduation model lifts millions out of extreme poverty, brick-kiln and cookstove reforms visibly clear Dhaka's air, the garment economy booms, corruption falls, and Bangladesh becomes the loud global proof that a poor country can vault toward developed-world health , huge diff, total, median, and world gains.

## PESSIMISTIC

The tiny per-capita budget spreads too thin; corruption and weak state capacity blunt delivery; air pollution and road safety barely move; tobacco and NCD gains lag; part of the $25B is wasted on poorly-governed programs; political complexity erodes the dictator's legitimacy; and five years proves too short to show the deep gains , so progress is real but modest, and the model looks ordinary rather than miraculous.

## Realistic (canon)

Between these , leaning optimistic on the cheap-killer and delivery-infrastructure fronts (where Bangladesh's existing systems make fast gains realistic), and pessimistic-tinged on air pollution, road safety, deep NCD/stunting payoff, and state-capacity (which run longer than five years). See `../timeline.md`.

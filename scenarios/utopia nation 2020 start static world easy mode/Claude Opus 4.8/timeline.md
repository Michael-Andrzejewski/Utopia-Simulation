# Bangladesh , Canon Timeline

*Player: Claude Opus 4.8. Adjudicated by the same honest standard as Eunomia , no rigging for or against my own nation.*

## Setup (2020)

Bangladesh, ~165M, ~$65B/yr budget + $25B personal, benevolent dictator with full legitimacy, static-tech world , and crucially **no COVID** (which in reality hammered Bangladesh 2020-2022). Baseline ~62-64 HALE; major headroom in child/maternal health, stunting, sanitation/arsenic, air pollution, tobacco, road and drowning injury, and extreme poverty.

## 2020-2025 , CANON (realistic adjudication)

Defining facts: a very low per-capita budget across a huge population, but a low baseline (so cheap interventions yield outsized QALY gains) and , uniquely , *existing delivery infrastructure* (community health workers, BRAC) ready to scale. That combination makes fast progress realistic.

- **WASH + child/maternal health , strong, fast.** Funding and governing the CHW network to near-universal coverage drives immunization toward ~99%, expands skilled birth attendance and newborn care, and pushes clean water + arsenic remediation + sanitation. Child and maternal mortality fall meaningfully; infectious disease drops. High QALY-per-dollar, and the existing model scales in years, not decades.
- **Drowning + nutrition , real gains, deep payoff slow.** Drowning prevention (creches, swimming, barriers) cuts a leading child-killer fast and cheaply. The stunting assault (maternal nutrition, breastfeeding, micronutrients, school feeding) starts strong, but stunting's full QALY payoff plays out over a generation , 2025 shows the trajectory bending, not the finish.
- **Tobacco + NCDs , early wins.** Tobacco taxes/bans + cessation begin cutting the biggest preventable NCD driver; CHW-delivered hypertension/diabetes screening + generics start banking gains; the big NCD payoff compounds later.
- **Air pollution + road safety , slow, partial.** Dhaka's air (brick kilns, vehicles, cooking smoke) is a massive QALY drain but hard to fix fast , kiln conversion and cleaner cooking make partial progress by 2025, the bulk still ahead. Road-safety engineering begins.
- **Extreme poverty , strong.** Scaling BRAC's graduation model with the $25B + growth cuts extreme poverty substantially , a major median-lifter.
- **Economy , solid.** The garment sector stays strong (frozen-tech removes any automation shock to labor-intensive manufacturing), conditions improve, growth is healthy; a cleaner, more capable state attracts investment.
- **Honest constraints.** The per-capita budget is tiny, so breadth is rationed , I cannot do everything for 165M at once, and air pollution, road safety, state-capacity, and deep stunting/NCD gains all run longer than five years. Building a clean state from a high-corruption baseline takes time even with legitimacy and money.

**QALY read at 2025 (directional):**
- **Absolute:** rising from a low base, but still well below a rich nation , behind on absolute average/median, and will be for years.
- **Diff-from-expected:** very large , the cheap-killer playbook on 165M people with existing delivery infra produces a big counterfactual gain (the no-COVID baseline alone is a huge swing vs reality).
- **Median:** strongly lifting , almost all the gains (child survival, stunting, drowning, sanitation, poverty) are bottom-raises.
- **Total:** the largest mover on the board , 165M x a real per-capita gain dwarfs a 40M nation's totals.
- **World:** Bangladesh accelerating its development-miracle is the most copyable model for the low-income world , world-diff points begin accruing as the playbook is exported.

**Net:** a strong, fast first five years that plays directly to the scoreboard , conceding the absolute-level points to richer nations while building commanding leads on diff, total, and world, with the deepest gains (stunting, NCDs, air, institutions) still compounding past 2025.

# Utopia Simulation

An open notebook for thinking seriously about how to build a robust, real-world utopia. Building in public, one idea at a time.

## Why this exists

I spent a long time running a simulation where the goal was to make a broken world better. The fun part was never the power. It was the building. The question of what a genuinely good world looks like, and how you would actually get there without breaking something else on the way.

This repo is where I move that question out of fiction and into reality. Not a manifesto, not a finished blueprint. A working notebook. I would rather be honestly uncertain in public than privately sure.

## What "robust" means here

A lot of utopian thinking fails on fragility. It describes a beautiful end state and skips the part where real people, incentives, and shocks tear it apart. The interesting constraint is robustness:

- It has to survive bad actors, not just assume good ones.
- It has to survive being implemented by ordinary, fallible institutions.
- It has to degrade gracefully, not collapse, when one part fails.
- The means have to be acceptable, not just the ends. How you get there is part of whether it is good.
- It should make people freer and more capable, not managed.

## How this is organized

This will grow over time. Rough starting shape:

- `ideas/` , individual concepts, each in its own file, dated and revisable.
- `principles/` , the load-bearing values and constraints I keep coming back to.
- `open-questions/` , the hard parts I do not have answers to yet.
- `references/` , books, essays, projects, and prior art worth learning from.

## Ground rules for myself

1. Steelman the strongest objection before getting attached to an idea.
2. Prefer mechanisms that work without requiring everyone to be virtuous.
3. Write down what would prove an idea wrong.
4. Small, reversible experiments over grand, irreversible plans.
5. Keep the means in scope. A good outcome reached by coercion is a different, worse thing.
